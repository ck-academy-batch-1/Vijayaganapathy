Did you know that the Great Barrier Reef in Australia is the largest living structure on Earth? It spans over 2,300 km and is made up of billions of coral polyps, algae, and other organisms. It's so massive that it can even be seen from space. The Great Barrier Reef is home to a diverse array of marine life and is an important habitat for many threatened species. Unfortunately, it is also facing significant threats from climate change and human activity, which have caused widespread coral bleaching and degradation.
